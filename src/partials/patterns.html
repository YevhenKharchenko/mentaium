Designing Focus: 5 Patterns Games Use to Guide Mental State

Exploring first-person games that exhale for you — literally.

Each pattern includes:

  • Use cases

  • Emotional response spectrum

  • Linked examples in known titles

  • Cross-reference to glossary terms

We catalogue recurring focus structures across games:

  • Tunnel Pattern – Narrow visual funneling (e.g. subway sequences, cave runs)

  • Loop Absorption – Small, repeatable interactions that induce trance (fishing, farming, orbit puzzles)

  • Ritualized Delay – Requiring players to wait before they act (day timers, charge builds)

  • Asymmetrical Silence – Unscored moments forcing interpretation

  • Micro-interruption Streams – Dialogs or cues that prevent full immersion
